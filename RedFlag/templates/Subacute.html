Subacute Back Pain

Symptoms :
1. longer than six weeks, but less than three months, of back pain.
2. that varies in intensity and may be either steady or intermittent.
3. Back pain or stiffness, especially in the morning or after periods of inactivity.
4. mild to moderate restrictions on daily activities and range of motion.
5. Possibly present discomfort or accompanied muscular spasms in the affected area.
Causes :
1. Back muscle or ligament tension can result from excessive exercise, repetitive motions, or poor posture.
2. Disc degeneration: As people age, their spinal discs might break down, resulting in subacute back discomfort.
3. Facet joint dysfunction: The tiny joints between the vertebrae may swell or itch, causing discomfort.
4. Constant mechanical stress: Subacute back pain may be brought on by poor body mechanics, bad posture, or carrying too much weight on the spine.
5. Chronic inflammation and back discomfort can be brought on by inflammatory diseases including rheumatoid arthritis and ankylosing spondylitis.
Risk Factors :
1. Lifestyle of inactivity: A sedentary lifestyle can weaken back muscles and raise the risk of subacute back pain.
2. Poor posture: Sitting or standing for extended periods of time in a stooped or slouched position can strain the back.
3. Obesity: Carrying around extra weight puts additional strain on the spine, which can cause back pain.
4. Risks associated with some occupations include those that require heavy lifting, repetitive motions, or extended periods of sitting or standing.
5. Psychological factors: Subacute back pain may develop or continue as a result of stress, anxiety, or depression.
Treatments :
1. Physical therapy: Specialised exercises, stretches, and strengthening methods can help with posture, back strength, and flexibility.
2. medications used to treat pain and inflammation include nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory medications (NSAIDs) and muscle relaxants.
3. Applying heat or cold therapy to the affected area can assist to lessen discomfort and swelling.
4. Manual therapy: To relieve pain and realign the spine, methods like chiropractic adjustments or osteopathic manipulative therapy may be utilised.
5. Cognitive-behavioral treatment (CBT): CBT can teach coping mechanisms and address any psychological issues causing subacute back pain.
6. A low-voltage electrical current is used in transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) therapy to treat pain.
7. For temporary pain relief, epidural steroid injections or nerve blocks may be used in specific circumstances.
8. Lifestyle changes: Adopting a healthy lifestyle, which includes regular exercise, weight control, and ergonomic upgrades, can encourage healing and help prevent recurrence.
